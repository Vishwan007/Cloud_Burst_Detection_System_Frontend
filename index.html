Understanding Cloud Bursts

Learn about the science behind cloud bursts, their causes, and how to stay safe during extreme weather events

What is a Cloud Burst?

A cloud burst is an extreme weather phenomenon characterized by sudden, intense rainfall over a small geographical area in a short period of time. It typically involves precipitation rates exceeding 100mm per hour.

These events are particularly dangerous because of their rapid onset and the massive volume of water they can dump in minutes, leading to flash floods and severe property damage.

Key Characteristics:

  • Rainfall rate > 100mm/hour
  • Duration: 15-30 minutes typically
  • Affects area of 20-30 sq km
  • Often accompanied by hail and lightning

Causes and Formation Process

Orographic Effect

Warm, moist air is forced upward by mountains, cooling rapidly and condensing into intense precipitation.

Convective Heating

Intense surface heating creates strong updrafts that carry water vapor high into the atmosphere.

High Moisture Content

Abundant water vapor in the atmosphere provides the raw material for heavy precipitation.

Safety Guidelines and Prevention

Before Cloud Burst

  • Monitor weather forecasts and warnings regularly
  • Prepare emergency kit (food, water, flashlight, radio)
  • Identify higher ground and evacuation routes
  • Keep important documents in waterproof containers
  • Install early warning systems and apps

During Cloud Burst

  • Never try to walk or drive through flooded areas
  • Stay indoors and move to higher floors
  • Keep communication devices charged and ready
  • Avoid electrical appliances and metal objects
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for updates

After Cloud Burst

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Inspect your home for structural damage
  • Avoid contaminated floodwater
  • Document damage for insurance claims
  • Help neighbors and community members
  • Report emergencies to authorities
